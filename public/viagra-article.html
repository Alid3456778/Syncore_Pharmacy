1. Mechanism of Action

The active component sildenafil in Viagra functions through its ability to block the enzyme phosphodiesterase type 5 (PDE5). The enzyme breaks down cyclic guanosine monophosphate (cGMP), which causes the blood vessel smooth muscle cells to relax. The relaxation of these cells enables increased blood flow to the penis, which functions as the fundamental mechanism for developing and sustaining an erection.

Erectile dysfunction causes patients to lose their ability to create a strong erection because their blood flow to the penis decreases from various medical conditions which include vascular disorders and neurological disorders and psychological disorders. The drug increases cGMP levels in penile tissues through its PDE5 inhibition mechanism which activates during sexual stimulation. The treatment increases blood circulation which leads to the development of an erection that helps men who experience erectile dysfunction treatment. The user needs to realize that Viagra does not create an erection which requires sexual stimulation for the medication to work properly.

Users should observe their body's reaction to Viagra 200 mg because it holds vital importance for their health.
